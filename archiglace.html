ARCHIGLACE


INSTALLATION
Musee Nationale de Beaux Arts, Quebec City
Miriam Ho in collaboration with F_RMLAB

Archiglace is a fibre and ice sculpture that captures the changeable properties of water in a material state of freezing. Inspired by the variegated fibres of a lemon fruit which form a graduated system of a continuous skin that dissolves into thick, tightly clinging pulp segments, the sculpture enlarges these textures for human perception and tactile material connection. Installed outdoors for the Quebec Winter Carnival, the sculpture invites visitors to enter a soft enclosure where they can experience the material environment through the sense of touch, an environment of variegated textures made possible by Canada's harsh winter conditions.

Archiglace was exhibited at the Musee Nationale de Beaux Arts in Quebec City in 2013
