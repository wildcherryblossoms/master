© ml projects 2019

ml projects is a collaboration between Lucas Smith and Miriam Ho.

Our artistic practice is driven by an inquiry into the socio-cultural dimensions of spatial practices. Our work lies at the intersection of film and architecture, drawing on our respective backgrounds in each discipline.

Lucas Smith is an American filmmaker. Born in Arizona, the desert landscapes, abandoned mining towns and urban sprawl of the American Southwest informed his worldview as well as artistic and social interests at an early age. In 2012, the IFP at Lincoln Center listed lucas as one of the top "Emerging Visions" in film. He was also a 2012 Fellow at the New York Film Festival's Artists Academy.

Miriam Ho is an architect, installation artist, writer and researcher. She uses the tools of an architect to work in the medium of drawing and writing as social and spatial critique. She also creates responsive sculptures and spatial installations to explore the potential of new materials and motion in space. Her work has been exhibited solo and collectively in Canada and abroad, including the GAIA Gallery in Gwangju, South Korea and the Musee Nationale de Beaux-Arts in Quebec City, Canada.
